Government Schemes

Explore various government healthcare schemes designed to provide financial assistance and support for medical treatments. Click on "Learn More" to visit the official websites for detailed information.

Ayushman Bharat Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (AB-PMJAY)

Provides a health cover of Rs. 5 lakhs per family per year for secondary and tertiary care hospitalization across public and private empaneled hospitals.

Learn More

Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana (PMSBY)

Offers accidental death and disability cover of Rs. 2 lakhs at a very low premium of Rs. 12 per annum.

Learn More

Pradhan Mantri Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana (PMJJBY)

A life insurance scheme offering a renewable one-year term life cover of Rs. 2 lakhs for death due to any reason, at an annual premium of Rs. 330.

Learn More

Rashtriya Swasthya Bima Yojana (RSBY)

A government-sponsored health insurance scheme for the unorganized sector workers falling under the BPL category.

Learn More

Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK)

Entitles all pregnant women delivering in public health institutions to absolutely free and no expense delivery, including C-section.

Learn More

National Health Mission (NHM)

Encompasses two sub-missions, the National Rural Health Mission (NRHM) and the National Urban Health Mission (NUHM), to provide accessible, affordable, and quality health care.

Learn More

Employees' State Insurance Scheme (ESIS)

A self-financing social security and health insurance scheme for Indian workers, providing medical, sickness, maternity, and disablement benefits.

Learn More

Central Government Health Scheme (CGHS)

Provides comprehensive medical care to Central Government employees, pensioners, and their dependent family members.

Learn More

Rashtriya Bal Swasthya Karyakram (RBSK)

An initiative to screen children from birth to 18 years for 4 Ds - Defects at birth, Deficiencies, Diseases, and Developmental Delays including Disabilities.

Learn More
